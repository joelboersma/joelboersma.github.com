Part Two

1. What is this image about?

David Hoang is my partner for this assignment. The image he gave me is a screenshot from the video game Minecraft. His character is standing next to a body of water and looking at a forest on the other side. David installed a certain type of mod, called a "shader mod", that makes his game look much more realistic and pleasing to the eye than it would otherwise.

2. What are the most interesting aspects of this image? Describe using some of the language in the introduction to this assignment.

To me, it seems like the purpose of this image is to showcase the shader mod that David installed. The environmental lighting and the surface of the water look much more beautiful than they would without the mod installed. The way that the water reflects the environment around it is only possible with this sort of mod installed. This location was probably chosen to show off the water reflections specifically.

The fact that there are no other players in this image may indicate that David is playing in singleplayer. Singleplayer Minecraft often evokes the feeling of trying to survive on your own.

3. What are the most obvious and the most mysterious aspects of this image?

The most obvious part of the image is that it looks like a very blocky world and that there is water, grass, and a forest. Those familiar with Minecraft would easily be able to recognize that this is a screenshot from the game, and many of them would instantly notice that there is a shader mod installed.

The most mysterious part of the image to me is the fact that it is extremely compressed and low resolution. While I understand that we need to optimize for the web, this may have gone too far to the point that there is a very noticeable quality drop. It is more difficult to appreciate the detail put into the lighting and water effects when the image is this compressed. It makes me wonder if there is some outside reason for the low image quality.

Beyond that, another mystery is whether this Minecraft world already existed before David downloaded the mod or if he created this world specifically to test the mod.

Article: "10 Intriguing Photographs to Teach Close Reading and Visual Thinking Skills"
Visual Thinking Website: Flipaclip

Flipaclip is an animation app for iOS and Android that is geared toward both beginners and professionals. But this journal entry is about their website.

The Flipaclip home page plays several animations as the user scrolls up or down the page. Many of them emphasize the features of the app, from animation tools to sharing features to in-app contests and learning resources.

One such animation shows a frame-by-frame hand-drawn animation of a bouncing ball paired with the caption "Simple. Intuitive. Fun." As the user continues scrolling, the animation later transitions to the ball bouncing within the app's interface, as if a user had drawn it. This animation helps highlight how easy the app is to use and helps the user feel like they can use the app too.

Other user-created animations shown on the page, such as the hand-drawn animation near the bottom and the rotoscoping example, are much more complex. These are shown to appeal to more experienced artists.

Some of the animations cause the scrolling to seemingly halt the page's vertical momentum. This encourages the user to focus on the content of that animation instead of scrolling past it. However, I do not feel that this is good UX design. When a user scrolls on a page, they usually expect upward or downward movement, but these animations break that flow, which may lead to user confusion. Other companies such as Apple have used similar techniques, and I personally am not a fan.

Form Design

April 14, 2021

1

Article: "Best Practices for Form Design"

This article highlighted many important considerations for form design that I had never thought of before. I believe that part of the reason that I had never considered these is this: sometimes the best product design is so seamless and straightforward that the user barely needs to think about it. Form design is no exception to this theory.

My primary focus when reading this article was how I could apply these principles to Studio 1: Mad Libs 2.0. A few possiblities are listed below.

  • One-Column Layout: For easier reading and faster form completion. Multiple sections will likely be unnecessary due to the small size of the form.
  • Label Position: Labels will be placed above each input field for easier reading and faster form completion.
  • Placeholder Text: Likely won't be necessary for this project.
  • Field Sizes: May size fields differently depending on context. For example, number fields may be smaller than word fields.
  • Auto-focus First Input Field: To clearly indicate the starting point and remove one tap/click.
  • Tab-readiness: I personally use the Tab key to move through text fields all the time, so I want to make sure this form works with this method.
  • Action button: The action button to end form entry will need to have a descriptive label, such as "Generate Mad Lib". The button may also need to be disabled on click.
  • Error Messages: Error messages will display beneath each input field and need to be concise and specific.
